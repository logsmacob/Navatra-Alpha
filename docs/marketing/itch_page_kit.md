### Long (Detailed description)
**Navatra is a single-player dice roguelite where each round is a high-stakes optimization puzzle.** There are no enemies and no traditional combat loop—your challenge is to reduce an escalating score quota to zero before your playable hands run out.

You roll 5 dice, lock what you want to keep, and reroll the rest—but rerolls are shared across the round, not refreshed every hand. That single rule turns every decision into risk management: push for perfect value now, or conserve resources for the final turns.

Scoring is built on reusable hand types, base values, and additive multipliers. As runs progress, your upgrades and trinkets shape a scoring engine that either outruns the quota curve—or collapses under it.

If you enjoy number-driven strategy, tight resource management, and “just one more run” progression, Navatra is built for you.

## "How to Play" (Player-Facing Tutorial Copy)

### Objective
Clear the round by reducing the **Quota** to **0** before you run out of **Hands**.

### Turn Flow
1. Roll all 5 dice.
2. Click/mark dice you want to keep.
3. Reroll the rest (if rerolls remain).
4. Submit your hand.
5. Your best valid hand type is scored automatically.
6. That score is subtracted from the round quota.

### Key Resources
- **Hands Remaining**: how many plays are left this round.
- **Rerolls Remaining**: shared across the full round.
- **Quota Remaining**: target you must reduce to zero.

### Win / Lose
- **Win the round**: Quota reaches 0.
- **Lose the run**: Hands reach 0 while quota is still above 0.

### Beginner Tips
- Don’t spend rerolls too early unless the hand is high value.
- Mid-value hands are often better than chasing perfect hands.
- Save at least one reroll for late-round recovery.
- Build upgrades that improve consistency, not just top-end spikes.
